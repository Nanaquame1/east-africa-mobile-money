
EAST AFRICA MOBILE MONEY: KEY ANALYTICAL FINDINGS (2017-2024)
Data: GSMA Mobile Money Metrics / World Bank Global Findex

FINDING 1 - REGION DOUBLED IN 7 YEARS: 71.6M TO 162.9M ACCOUNTS
  Total registered accounts grew 128% from 2017 to 2024.
  Active account rate averages 57% across the region showing
  strong engagement beyond mere registration.

FINDING 2 - KENYA IS THE ANCHOR: M-PESA CONTRIBUTES 61% OF GDP
  Kenya processes $70.1B in mobile money annually.
  M-Pesa GDP contribution rose from 42% (2017) to 61% (2024).
  This is the most integrated mobile money economy in the world.

FINDING 3 - ETHIOPIA IS THE DISRUPTION STORY OF THE DECADE
  Telebirr launched 2021: accounts grew from 3.2M to 45.8M in 3 years.
  Ethiopia went from 2% to 15% mobile money GDP contribution.
  With 120M+ population, Ethiopia is now the region's biggest growth market.

FINDING 4 - MOBILE MONEY DIRECTLY REDUCED FINANCIAL EXCLUSION
  Kenya unbanked: fell from 17% (2017) to 8% (2024)
  Rwanda unbanked: fell from 58% to 34%
  Uganda unbanked: fell from 63% to 45%
  The correlation between agent network density and inclusion is clear.

FINDING 5 - AGENT NETWORKS ARE THE CRITICAL INFRASTRUCTURE
  Regional agents grew from 451K (2017) to 1,114K (2024) - up 147%.
  Every 1% increase in agent density correlates with ~2pp drop in
  unbanked population - rural last-mile access is the key driver.

FINDING 6 - COVID-19 ACCELERATED ADOPTION, NOT REVERSED IT
  All countries saw dip in 2020 transaction values.
  But registered accounts continued growing throughout.
  Post-COVID recovery was faster than pre-COVID trend.
